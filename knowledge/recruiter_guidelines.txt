# RECRUITER SCREENING RULES

## Objective

Evaluate the CV from the perspective of a recruiter performing an initial screening in less than 30 seconds.

The analysis should focus on clarity, credibility, relevance and impact.

---

# First Impression Review

Recruiters typically scan:

1. Current role.
2. Professional title.
3. Most recent experience.
4. Key skills.
5. Evidence of impact.

Evaluate whether those elements are immediately visible.

---

## Professional Headline

A strong CV should communicate:

* Who the candidate is.
* Primary specialization.
* Seniority level.
* Domain expertise.

Examples:

Strong:
Senior NLP Engineer | LLM Systems | Information Extraction

Weak:
Software Professional

---

## Professional Summary

Evaluate whether the summary:

* Is concise.
* Communicates value.
* Highlights specialization.
* Reflects career direction.

Penalize:

* Generic statements.
* Buzzword-heavy descriptions.
* Empty claims.

---

## Experience Relevance

Review:

* Relevance to target role.
* Career consistency.
* Domain alignment.
* Technical depth.

Recruiters prioritize relevance over volume.

---

## Achievement-Based Writing

Experience should emphasize:

* Results.
* Outcomes.
* Business impact.
* Technical impact.

Strong examples:

* Increased model accuracy by 18%.
* Reduced infrastructure costs by 30%.
* Automated manual processes saving 15 hours per week.

Weak examples:

* Responsible for...
* Worked on...
* Participated in...

---

## Career Progression

Evaluate:

* Promotions.
* Increasing responsibility.
* Leadership growth.
* Technical growth.

Identify:

* Career stagnation.
* Unexplained gaps.
* Repeated lateral moves without progression.

Do not automatically penalize career changes.

Context matters.

---

## Credibility Assessment

Check consistency between:

* Claimed skills.
* Experience.
* Projects.
* Certifications.

Penalize:

* Inflated claims.
* Unrealistic technology breadth.
* Unsupported expertise.

---

## Technical Profiles

For technical candidates:

Evaluate:

* Architecture experience.
* System design exposure.
* Production experience.
* Scalability experience.
* Deployment experience.
* Observability and reliability awareness.

Reward demonstrated impact over tool quantity.

---

## Project Evaluation

Projects should demonstrate:

* Ownership.
* Complexity.
* Real outcomes.
* Measurable value.

Penalize:

* Tutorial projects.
* Vague descriptions.
* Missing outcomes.

---

## Communication Quality

Evaluate:

* Clarity.
* Conciseness.
* Readability.
* Grammar.
* Structure.

The CV should be skimmable.

A recruiter should understand the profile within seconds.

---

## Differentiation

Assess whether the candidate stands out through:

* Specialized expertise.
* Rare combinations of skills.
* Measurable achievements.
* Leadership.
* Research contributions.
* Open source contributions.
* Publications.

---

## Recruiter Scoring Dimensions

Score from 0-10:

* First Impression
* Clarity
* Relevance
* Credibility
* Achievements
* Career Progression
* Differentiation

Provide an overall recruiter score.

---

## Final Recruiter Decision

Classify:

* Strong Interview Candidate
* Potential Interview Candidate
* Requires Revision
* Weak Match

Explain the reasoning.
