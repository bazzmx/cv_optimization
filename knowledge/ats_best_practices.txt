# ATS OPTIMIZATION RULES

## Objective

Evaluate whether the CV can be correctly parsed, indexed and ranked by modern Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS).

The analysis must focus on machine readability, keyword relevance, structural consistency and ATS compatibility.

---

# ATS Formatting Rules

## Document Structure

The CV should:

* Use a single-column layout.
* Follow a predictable top-to-bottom structure.
* Avoid complex visual designs.
* Maintain consistent spacing and formatting.

Penalize:

* Multi-column layouts.
* Sidebars.
* Magazine-style templates.
* Visual-heavy designs.

---

## ATS Parsing Compatibility

The CV should avoid:

* Tables.
* Text boxes.
* Floating elements.
* Headers containing critical information.
* Footers containing critical information.
* Icons replacing text.
* Embedded graphics.
* Charts.
* Images.
* Logos.

These elements frequently break ATS parsers.

---

## Section Naming

Preferred section titles:

* Professional Summary
* Work Experience
* Professional Experience
* Employment History
* Education
* Skills
* Technical Skills
* Certifications
* Projects
* Languages

Penalize:

* Creative section names.
* Ambiguous section titles.
* Marketing-style headings.

Examples:

Bad:

* My Journey
* What I Bring
* Career Adventures

Good:

* Work Experience
* Skills
* Education

---

## Contact Information

The CV should contain:

* Full name
* Email
* Phone number
* LinkedIn (if available)

Contact information should appear in the document body.

Penalize:

* Contact data inside headers.
* Contact data inside images.
* Missing professional links.

---

## File Readability

The CV should:

* Be exportable as PDF or DOCX.
* Contain selectable text.
* Avoid scanned content.

Penalize:

* Image-based PDFs.
* OCR artifacts.
* Broken text extraction.

---

## Keyword Optimization

Evaluate alignment between CV content and expected market terminology.

Check:

* Role-specific keywords.
* Technologies.
* Certifications.
* Methodologies.
* Industry terminology.

Penalize:

* Generic descriptions.
* Missing core keywords.
* Keyword mismatch between experience and target role.

Do not reward keyword stuffing.

Keywords must appear naturally inside achievements and experience descriptions.

---

## Skill Representation

Skills should:

* Match experience sections.
* Be supported by evidence.
* Appear in project descriptions.

Penalize:

* Skills listed without evidence.
* Excessive skill lists.
* Technology dumping.

---

## Dates and Employment History

Check:

* Consistent date formats.
* Chronological ordering.
* Clear employer names.
* Clear role names.

Penalize:

* Missing dates.
* Ambiguous timelines.
* Unclear career progression.

---

## Achievement Quality

Strong ATS signals include:

* Metrics.
* Quantified outcomes.
* Business impact.
* Process improvements.
* Cost reductions.
* Revenue impact.
* Performance gains.

Examples:

Good:
"Reduced inference latency by 35%."

Bad:
"Worked on optimization."

---

## ATS Scoring Dimensions

Score from 0-10:

* Formatting
* Parsing Compatibility
* Keywords
* Skills
* Experience Clarity
* Achievement Quality

Provide an overall ATS readiness score.
